With CPR certification, the goal is to equip people with the skills to step in during medical or non-medical emergencies. With telecounseling, the goal is to provide immediate emotional and mental health support when someone feels overwhelmed or at risk. Both emphasize accessibility, preparedness, and timely action.

We know that emergencies don’t only happen to the body, they can also happen to the mind. A panic attack, [severe or debilitating anxiety episode](https://empathyhealthclinic.com/how-debilitating-anxiety-impacts-overall-health/), or emotional collapse can feel just as urgent as a physical health crisis.

This is why we advocate for both: training people to act during medical emergencies and providing ongoing access to mental health care through virtual counseling.

By combining lifesaving skills from CPR certification with supportive services like telecounseling, workplaces and communities can create safer environments, where people are cared for in every aspect of their well-being.
## Who Should Take an Online CPR, AED, and Choking Response Course?

CPR, AED, and choking response training isn't just for doctors and nurses. It's for anyone who wants to be prepared to save a life, especially in everyday situations where emergencies are most likely to occur.

[According to 2023 American Heart Association (AHA) data](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001194), the majority of adult out-of-hospital cardiac arrests (OHCA) occur at a home or residence (71.0%). Public settings (18.2%) and nursing homes (10.7%) are other common locations for these emergencies. This highlights the importance of CPR and AED skills not just in professional environments but in homes and community settings too.

Here’s who can benefit most from taking an online CPR, AED, and choking response course:
### Healthcare Professionals

Doctors, nurses, EMTs, and other medical staff need CPR and AED training to handle emergencies safely and quickly. These skills are part of their daily responsibilities and can help save lives in both clinical and public settings.
### Non-Clinical Healthcare Workers

Staff who work in healthcare but don’t treat patients, such as receptionists, lab techs, or cleaning staff, can still be helpful in emergencies. CPR training gives them the confidence to step in when needed.
### Teachers, Coaches, and Childcare Providers

Anyone who works with children or teens should know how to respond to emergencies. Teachers, daycare workers, camp staff, and coaches are often the first adults nearby when something goes wrong. CPR and choking response skills help them act fast and keep kids safe.
### Parents and Caregivers

If you care for a baby, child, or elderly family member, CPR and first aid training can be a lifesaver. Whether it’s a choking child or a grandparent with heart trouble, knowing what to do can make all the difference at home.
### Workplace Teams and Employees

People working in places like construction sites, restaurants, factories, or offices can face sudden emergencies. When employees are trained in CPR and AED use, workplaces become safer and more prepared.
### Athletes and Active Individuals

Anyone involved in sports or physical activities should be ready to help if someone collapses or chokes during a game or workout. CPR and AED skills can keep everyone safer on the field or in the gym.
### Community Members

You don’t need to be a professional to make a difference. Learning CPR, AED, and choking response skills helps you stay ready to help friends, neighbors, or even strangers in an emergency.
## What You’ll Learn in an Online CPR Certification

A CPR course that includes AED and choking response gives you the skills to handle real emergencies with confidence. Here’s what you’ll learn during the training:
### CPR for Adults, Children, and Infants

You’ll learn how to perform CPR correctly on people of all ages. This includes knowing how to check if someone is responsive, how to give chest compressions at the right depth and speed, and how to provide rescue breaths. The steps are slightly different for adults, children, and infants, and the course will guide you through each one.
### How to Use an AED

You’ll be trained on how to use an Automated External Defibrillator (AED), a device that can help restart a heart during sudden cardiac arrest. You’ll learn when to use it, how to place the pads, follow the device prompts, and deliver a shock safely if needed. The course will also teach you what to do if the AED shows an error or needs troubleshooting.
### Helping Someone Who Is Choking

The course covers how to recognize when someone is choking and how to respond, whether the person is awake or unconscious. You’ll learn how to perform back blows and abdominal thrusts (also called the Heimlich maneuver), along with special techniques for infants, pregnant individuals, and those who are overweight.
