If applicable, the professional will issue an emotional support animal letter, typically for a fee ranging between $100 and $200.

The timeline for receiving the letter varies. Patients often receive their ESA letter after their initial evaluation, but this can take up to two weeks if further assessment or additional appointments are necessary.

## Legal Protections for Emotional Support Animals in Florida

In Florida, individuals with an emotional support animal letter are protected under the Fair Housing Act (FHA) and the Air Carrier Access Act (ACAA). These laws prevent discrimination against individuals with disabilities that are helped by ESAs. Under the FHA, landlords must accommodate tenants who own ESAs, even if the residence has a 'no pets' policy. However, landlords can require proof of need, usually verified through a valid ESA letter.

The ACAA previously allowed ESAs to travel in airplane cabins, but as of new regulations in 2021, this has changed, allowing airlines to categorize ESAs as pets for flights. Check with specific airlines before travel, as policies are now airline-specific. Florida law also prohibits fees or deposits for ESAs in housing but does allow landlords to charge for any damages caused.

Overall, while ESAs enjoy certain protections, they are not given the same access as service animals. Misrepresentation of an animal as an ESA is illegal in Florida, potentially resulting in hefty fines.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid When Getting an ESA Letter

Obtaining an ESA letter can be straightforward if done correctly, but common mistakes can invalidate the letter. One frequent issue is using online services that promise instant ESA letters without a proper mental health evaluation. These can often be fraudulent or not recognized by landlords or housing officials.

To avoid such scams, ensure the evaluation includes a thorough review by a licensed mental health professional rather than a generic online questionnaire. Additionally, confirm that your letter is legitimate by verifying the provider's credentials.

In Orlando, some platforms offer telehealth options, allowing legitimate assessments via video consultations. Confirm with your landlord or housing provider if they accept these formats. Misrepresenting your health status to obtain an ESA letter is illegal in Florida and can lead to penalties, including fines or eviction.

## How to Use Your Emotional Support Animal Letter

After receiving your emotional support animal letter, understanding its application is crucial. For housing, present the letter to your landlord or housing authority, especially if their policies typically restrict pets. The letter should outline your need for an ESA and be from a licensed practitioner.

While your ESA letter grants certain allowances, it does not spare you from landlord obligations such as maintaining cleanliness and preventing animal disturbances. Additionally, while the FHA supports ESA accommodations, the letter must be current, typically renewed annually, to maintain its validity.

If traveling, especially by air, verify with your airline how they categorize ESAs as many no longer consider them service animals. This can result in ESAs having to travel as checked pets, resulting in additional fees. Being informed can prevent last-minute issues and ensure a smooth process.
