## How EMDR Therapy Works
EMDR therapy uses bilateral stimulation through guided eye movements to help your brain process traumatic memories differently. During sessions with a qualified **therapist near me**, you will work through disturbing memories while following specific eye movement patterns that facilitate natural healing processes in the brain.

The theory behind EMDR suggests that trauma can become stuck in the brain when overwhelming experiences are not properly processed during the initial traumatic event. A skilled **therapist near me** uses EMDR techniques to help your brain reprocess these memories, reducing their emotional impact and allowing for natural healing to occur over time.

Research from the [American Psychological Association](https://www.apa.org/ptsd-guideline/treatments/eye-movement-reprocessing) shows that EMDR can be as effective as traditional talk therapy for PTSD treatment, often with faster results and fewer sessions required. Our [experienced Orlando therapists](https://empathyhealthclinic.com/in-person-meaning-therapy-guide/) use evidence-based EMDR protocols to help clients process trauma safely and effectively.

The bilateral stimulation used in EMDR therapy activates both hemispheres of the brain, similar to what occurs naturally during REM sleep when memories are processed and integrated. This neurobiological process helps transform traumatic memories from their raw, overwhelming state into more manageable, integrated memories that no longer cause distress.

## The Eight Phases of EMDR Treatment
When you begin working with an EMDR **therapist near me**, treatment follows a structured eight-phase approach designed to ensure safety and effectiveness throughout the entire healing process from start to finish.

Phase 1 involves comprehensive history taking and treatment planning where your **therapist near me** will gather detailed information about your trauma history, current symptoms, and specific treatment goals. This phase includes careful assessment of your readiness for EMDR processing and identification of target memories that will be addressed during treatment sessions.

Phase 2 focuses on preparation and stabilization where you will learn essential coping skills and relaxation techniques to manage any distress that may arise during or between therapy sessions. This phase establishes a solid foundation of safety and emotional stability before any trauma processing begins with your qualified therapist.

Phase 3 involves detailed assessment where your therapist identifies the specific target memory and carefully evaluates various elements including the visual image, negative cognition, positive cognition, emotions, and body sensations associated with the traumatic memory that will be processed.

Phase 4 is the desensitization phase which involves the actual bilateral stimulation while you focus on the target memory until the memory no longer causes significant emotional distress or overwhelming feelings when recalled during the session.

Phase 5 focuses on installation where the positive cognition identified in phase 3 is strengthened and integrated to replace negative beliefs about yourself and the traumatic experience that may have developed over time.

Phase 6 involves a comprehensive body scan where your therapist carefully checks for any residual physical tension or discomfort related to the target memory to ensure complete processing has occurred.

Phase 7 ensures proper closure where each session ends with specific stabilization techniques to ensure you feel grounded, safe, and stable before leaving the therapy session and returning to your daily activities.

Phase 8 involves thorough reevaluation where your progress is carefully assessed at the beginning of each subsequent session to ensure treatment gains are maintained and to identify any new targets for processing in future sessions.
